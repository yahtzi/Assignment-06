Book Suggestions for Douglas


Halo: Fall of Reach

Eric Nylund | Del Rey

A space military science fiction novel, set int the Halo universe, acting as a prequel to Halo:Combat evolved. Released in October 2001, events take place in the Halo universe, traveling faster than the speed of light is possible through slipspace, another dimension where special relativity does not apply. This has allowed humans to colonize hundreds of other planets which are controlled by the United Nations Space Command (UNSC). Feeling repressed by the UNSC's heavy-handed rule, some colonies revolt. Fearing rebellion will tear the UNSC apart, military leaders approve the SPARTAN-II Program, a secret squad of super soldiers designed to quietly suppress rebellion.

A Child Called It

Dave Pelzer | Deerfield Beach

A Child Called It, is an autobiography of a young boy who is starved, beaten, and tortured by his mother. David was beaten everyday as a child. He was skinny because he was constantly deprived of food, and was forced to be the family's slave. As times passes, David's punishments became more cruel and dangerous. David was forced to wear the same clothing each day, which made him an outcast at school because he smelled bad. This was just one way his mother humiliated him. David uses his courage and internal motivation to come out of the dark times and make a life for himself, which included a great deal of public speaking.

The Giver

Lois Lowry | Houghton Mifflin

Jonas, a 12-year-old boy, lives in a communitiy isolated from all except a few similar towns, where everyone from small infants to the Chief Elder has an assigned role. The day finally arrives, and Jonas is assembled with his classmates in order of birth. All of the community is present, and the Chief Elder preside. Nervous, Jonas is stunned when his turn is passed by, and he is increasingly conspicuous and agonized until he is alone. The Chief Elder then explains that Jonas has not been given a normal assignment, but instead ahs been selected as the next Reciever of Memory, to be trained by the current one, who sits among the Elders, and shares the boy unusual pale eyes. Rules and responsibility separate Jonas from his family and friends, it is then that he realizes the burden of being the carrier of all history, resulting in conflicting choices throughout the book.
